# Information Sheet & Consent Form — MarineSABRES SES Toolbox Pilot Study

## Study title

Evaluation of AI-assisted Social-Ecological Systems Modelling in the
MarineSABRES SES Toolbox: A pilot study.

## Funding

Horizon Europe MarineSABRES (Grant 101059482) and MARBEFES
(Grant 101060937).

## What this study is about

You are invited to participate in a small pilot evaluation of a web-based
software tool called the MarineSABRES SES Toolbox. The toolbox helps
researchers build models of how human activities, environmental
pressures, ecosystem processes, and human well-being interact in marine
settings, using the DAPSI(W)R(M) framework.

We have built a version of the toolbox that uses machine learning to
suggest connections between model elements. We do not yet know whether
those suggestions actually help users. This study answers that question.

## What we are asking you to do

You will be asked to use the toolbox to build a model of a Mediterranean
coastal-lagoon social-ecological system, twice, on two different
occasions one week apart. Each session is 60 minutes total (45 minutes
of modelling + 15 minutes of onboarding and a workload questionnaire).

You will see one of two versions of the toolbox at each session, in a
randomized order:

- Version A: the baseline toolbox without ML suggestions.
- Version B: the same toolbox with the ML-suggestion features visible.

You will be told which version is which only after both sessions are
complete.

The two sessions will take place over a video call or in person, at
your convenience. You can stop at any time.

## What we will collect

- The model you build during each session (the toolbox saves this as
  a project file).
- The time you spent on each session, captured by the toolbox.
- Your responses to a standard workload questionnaire (NASA-TLX, six
  numeric sliders 0-100) at the end of each session.
- A hashed participant identifier so we can link your two sessions
  without knowing who you are at the analysis stage.

## What we will NOT collect

- Your name, email, IP address, or any contact details, beyond what is
  needed for scheduling the two sessions.
- Video or audio recordings.
- Keystroke-level data or screen recordings.

## How your data will be handled

- Your raw participant identifier (which is in your URL during the
  session, e.g. "P03") is hashed at the toolbox boundary before any
  data is written to disk. Only the first 12 characters of the SHA-256
  hash leave the browser. The mapping from your raw identifier to the
  hashed value is kept in a password-protected file by the PI and
  destroyed after data analysis.
- The model you build, the timing data, and the workload questionnaire
  responses are stored on a Klaipeda University server
  (laguna.ku.lt) under restricted access.
- Only aggregate, anonymised results will be reported in any
  publication. Individual model files will not be shared.

## Your rights

- You may withdraw at any time before, during, or after either session,
  without giving a reason. If you withdraw, your data will be deleted
  from our analysis dataset.
- You may ask us to delete your data at any point up to publication of
  the results, expected at ESP 2026 (October-November 2026).
- You can ask any question about the study at any time, and we will
  answer fully.

## Risks and benefits

- Risk: minimal. The task is computer-based, you are seated at your
  own workstation, and the modelling content is not stressful.
- Benefit to you: you get early access to a research tool and an
  opportunity to give feedback that will shape its development. We
  will share the pilot results with all participants before publication.
- Benefit to others: your participation helps establish whether AI
  assistance genuinely helps non-experts build marine ecosystem
  models.
